David believes in the power of cities. David also believes in the power of information to help people make smarter decisions about how they get around cities.

What David Does

David lives and breathes alternative* modes of transportation.

Walk if the destination is within a block or two. Bike if a destination is within a few miles. Take transit if the previous two conditions do not apply or if traveling with somebody who does not adhere to the above-said rules. Take Lyft if forced to use an auto.

David is willing to provide his services in almost any urban area in the world, as long as he does not need a car to get about, and as long as it's OK if he speaks English, since his 1.5 years of living in Belgium proved that he'd need a few millennia to learn a new language.

*The mass-produced automobile has been around for a little more than 100 years, whereas people have been around for about 2,000 times as long as that. Why (WHY?!!!) is a non-SOV mode of transportation labeled as 'alternative'?!!

How David started

David completed a BS in Civil Engineering in (year redacted) from the University of Arizona and an MS in Urban Planning in (year redacted), also from the University of Arizona. He completed a 6-month, full-stack web development program at Thinkful.

David worked as an urban planner in 4 communities and in 3 regions, and was self-employed as a dog trainer in the DC metro area. He is proud to say that, with the exception of a one-month transition upon acceptance of a new job, and the necessities of traveling to people's homes for dog training, David has never driven to work.

David attempts to adhere to a green lifestyle: minimal use of automobiles, minimal consumption of animal products, and heaping spoonfuls of "reduce, re-use, and recycle."

Who David Is

David believes in creating smarter, more sustainable cities, and wants to be hired by people who live these values every day. He will never drive to work, and loves the notion of working for a carbon-neutral company.

David supports open standards, and will be contributing to open data and open source efforts whenever he can.
